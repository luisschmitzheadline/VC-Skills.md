# SaaS Exit Strategies 2025-2026

## Exit Options Overview

### 1. Strategic Acquisition
**Best for:** Product/market synergies, quick exit
- 62% of LMM SaaS deals in 2025 (up from 55% in 2023)
- Typically highest multiples (7-12x ARR)
- Fastest close (3-6 months)

**Target Buyers:**
- Big Tech (Microsoft, Google, Salesforce, Adobe)
- Mid-Market Tech (consolidating verticals)
- Competitors (horizontal expansion)
- Adjacent Industry Players (vertical integration)

**What They Look For:**
- Product differentiation
- Customer base overlap/expansion
- Technology/IP acquisition
- Talent acquisition (acqui-hire)
- Market position defense

### 2. Private Equity (PE) Acquisition
**Best for:** Profitable, scaled businesses
- Active in buy-and-build strategies
- Consolidating niche verticals
- Typical 4-7x ARR multiples

**PE Preferences:**
- EBITDA positive or path to profitability
- >$5M ARR preferred
- Strong retention metrics
- Scalable operations
- Professional management

**Deal Structures:**
- Majority buyout (60-80%)
- Growth equity (20-40%)
- Recap with founder rollover

### 3. Search Fund / Individual Buyer
**Best for:** Small, profitable SaaS
- $1M-$5M ARR sweet spot
- 2-4x ARR typical multiple
- Longer process (6-12 months)

**What They Want:**
- Owner-operated potential
- Stable, recurring revenue
- Simple business model
- Growth opportunity
- <$2M purchase price ideal

### 4. IPO (Initial Public Offering)
**Best for:** Large, high-growth companies
- Typically >$100M ARR
- >40% growth rate
- Path to profitability
- Rare for bootstrapped companies

### 5. Management Buyout (MBO)
**Best for:** Founder succession
- Existing management takes over
- Often with PE backing
- 3-5x ARR typical

## Exit Timeline Planning

### Year -3 (36 months before exit)
```yaml
focus:
  - Establish clean financials (GAAP/audited)
  - Build management team (reduce founder dependency)
  - Document processes and systems
  - Fix technical debt
  - Resolve any legal/IP issues

metrics_targets:
  - ARR growth trajectory established
  - Unit economics proven
  - Customer concentration <25%
```

### Year -2 (24 months before exit)
```yaml
focus:
  - Optimize for key metrics (NRR, margin)
  - Build Board/advisors with M&A experience
  - Begin informal buyer conversations
  - Prepare data room

metrics_targets:
  - NRR >100%
  - Gross margin >70%
  - Rule of 40 trajectory
```

### Year -1 (12 months before exit)
```yaml
focus:
  - Engage M&A advisor/banker
  - Finalize data room
  - Run formal process
  - Negotiate and close

metrics_targets:
  - Hit all KPI targets
  - Clean audit
  - No surprises
```

## Deal Structure Components

### Purchase Price Allocation
| Component | Typical Range | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Cash at Close | 60-80% | Immediate liquidity |
| Earnout | 10-30% | Performance-based |
| Rollover Equity | 10-30% | Alignment |
| Escrow | 5-15% | Risk mitigation |

### Earnout Structures
```yaml
time_based:
  - Year 1: X% if stay
  - Year 2: X% if stay
  - Total: 10-25% of deal value

metric_based:
  - ARR target: If hit $X ARR by date
  - Revenue target: X% of excess revenue
  - Retention target: If NRR >X%

risk: High if aggressive targets
recommendation: Negotiate achievable targets
```

### Rollover Equity
- Typically 10-30% of proceeds
- Aligns founder with new owner
- Second bite of apple potential
- Tax deferral benefits
- Due diligence on new partner critical

## Maximizing Exit Value

### Multiple Boosters
| Action | Impact | Effort |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Improve NRR to >110% | +1-2x multiple | High |
| Achieve Rule of 40 | +1.1x per 10pts | Medium |
| Reduce churn to <2% monthly | +1x multiple | High |
| Diversify customer base | +0.5x multiple | Medium |
| Add AI capabilities | +1-2x multiple | High |
| Vertical specialization | +0.5-1x multiple | Low |
| Clean financials | +0.5x multiple | Medium |
| Reduce founder dependency | +0.5x multiple | High |

### Red Flags to Eliminate
| Red Flag | Multiple Impact |
|----------|-----------------|
| Single customer >25% revenue | -1-2x |
| Founder as only salesperson | -1x |
| No documented processes | -0.5x |
| Technical debt | -0.5-1x |
| Pending litigation | Deal killer |
| IP issues | Deal killer |
| High employee turnover | -0.5x |

## Negotiation Considerations

### LOI (Letter of Intent) Terms
- Purchase price (range acceptable)
- Deal structure (cash/stock/earnout)
- Exclusivity period (30-60 days typical)
- Due diligence scope
- Closing conditions
- Non-compete terms

### Key Negotiation Points
1. **Valuation**: Multiple of what metric (ARR, revenue, EBITDA)
2. **Working capital**: Target vs actual adjustment
3. **Earnout**: Targets, measurement, caps
4. **Escrow**: Amount, duration, release conditions
5. **Reps & warranties**: Scope and survival period
6. **Non-compete**: Duration and geography
7. **Employment**: Transition period requirements
